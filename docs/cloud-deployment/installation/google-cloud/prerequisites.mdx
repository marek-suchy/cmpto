Please make sure that you meet all the following requirements for using Compute Engine in Google Cloud before continuing to the next page.

### Creating a new Google Cloud project

1. Visit [cloud.google.com](https://cloud.google.com) and sign in to your Google account
2. Go to *Console* located in the top-right corner
3. In the top-left corner is a drop-down menu to manage your projects. Click on it and create a new project

![](/img/cloud-deployment/Screenshot_2023-05-19_at_9.54.09_AM.png)

### Enabling billing for a Google Cloud account

To enable billing in your project, please refer to the official Google Cloud [docs](https://cloud.google.com/billing/docs/how-to/modify-project#enable_billing_for_a_new_project).

### Enabling the Compute Engine service

1. On the dashboard, in the top-left corner, open-up menu
2. Select *APIs & Services* and then *Enabled APIs & services*

![](/img/cloud-deployment/apis.jpg)

3. Click on *Enable APIs and services* button

![](/img/cloud-deployment/Screenshot_2023-05-19_at_10.44.40_AM.png)

4. And finally, find the Compute Engine service and enable it

![](/img/cloud-deployment/Screenshot_2023-05-19_at_10.47.41_AM.png)

### Checking and requesting quotas

1. On the dashboard, in the top-left corner, open-up menu
2. Select *IAM & Admin* and then click on *Quotas*

![](/img/cloud-deployment/Screenshot_2023-05-19_at_11.04.41_AM.png)

3. On the Quotas page, filter out *Service: Compute Engine*, then find your quota for GPUs and CPUs
4. You may also filter out the region you'd like to set up the quota for
5. Once the filter is set, select filtered items by their checkboxes and click on *Edit Quotas*

![](/img/cloud-deployment/Screenshot_2023-05-19_at_10.57.27_AM.png)

6. In the popped-up dialog, specify the new limit for a particular quota and submit your request.

:::note Quota limits recommendation

**Minimum:**

* 4 vCPUs
* A single GPU

Note that you need at least 48 vCPUs to handle a single fully motion-compensated Framerate/Standards conversion stream at resolution 1920x1080 when converting from 50 FPS to 60 FPS or vice versa.
:::
