<div className="feature-detail__section">

AV Delay allows operators to introduce a configurable delay to either the audio or video path independently, compensating for timing mismatches that arise from different processing latencies in the signal chain. This ensures lip-sync accuracy across the entire production workflow.

## Key capabilities

Independent delay adjustment for audio and video paths. Millisecond-precision control. Applied in real time without stream interruption. Compensates for upstream processing latency differences between audio and video hardware.

## Broadcast engineering context

In professional broadcast chains, audio and video often travel through different processing stages — SDI embedders, frame synchronisers, hardware decoders, or software codecs — each introducing different amounts of latency. Without compensation, the encoded stream may arrive with a persistent lip-sync error. AV Delay corrects this at the encoder before the stream leaves the facility.

Typical sources of misalignment:
- Audio processing hardware with lower latency than video scalers
- Frame synchronisers introducing variable video delay
- Software decode paths with inconsistent processing times

## Use cases

- **Live events** — correct lip-sync before contribution to broadcast centre
- **Remote production** — compensate for SDI-to-IP conversion timing differences
- **On-prem studio** — align audio from console with video from camera chain

<UISettings>

Navigate to **Settings → Encoder → Input → AV Delay**. Available on all input types. Set a positive audio delay to postpone the audio relative to video, or a negative value to do the opposite. Both audio and video delay can be set independently in milliseconds.
